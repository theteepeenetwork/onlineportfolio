# StoryJar Launch Plan — September 2026

**Goal:** Convert Seesaw-fatigued UK teachers into StoryJar users for the autumn term (starts ~Tue 1 Sept 2026, pupils in by 2–3 Sept).
**Wedge:** Individual teachers, bottom-up. **Budget:** £0–500, solo. **Product status:** Beta, feature-freeze needed by mid-August.

---

## 1. Positioning

**One-liner:** *StoryJar — the simple way to capture children's learning. No AI push, no bloat, no per-pupil price creep. Just photos, voices and work, shared with parents in seconds.*

Market context (verified July 2026):

- Seesaw for Schools runs ~$7/student/year (3-yr consortium rate), plus $0.85/student for the AI upgrade they're pushing hard. A 300-pupil primary pays ~£1,700+/yr and gets AI features many teachers didn't ask for.
- Tapestry (the EYFS incumbent) starts at £68/yr, ~£99/yr for 40 children — proof UK schools respond to simple flat pricing with all features included.

**The angle:** don't attack Seesaw's size, attack its *drift*. "Remember when Seesaw just let you record learning? That's all StoryJar does — brilliantly." Every message reinforces: back to basics, teacher-first, no AI upsell.

Three proof points to repeat everywhere: capture in under 10 seconds; parents see work without app friction; one honest price, every feature included.

## 2. Pricing recommendation

Launch with **free for individual teachers (1 class), flat-priced for schools**:

- **Teacher (free):** 1 class, core capture + parent sharing. This is your growth engine — no card, no trial clock.
- **School (~£299/yr flat, or tiered £199/£299/£449 by pupil count):** unlimited classes, admin oversight, exports/transfer at year end. Undercuts Seesaw dramatically and mirrors Tapestry's trusted flat model.

Why not free trial: September adopters churn in October if a trial expires mid-term. A permanently free teacher tier keeps them in until their school buys. Decide final numbers by 1 August — the landing page needs them.

## 3. Timeline

### Phase 1 — Finish & seed (18 July – 10 Aug)

- **Product:** ruthless scope cut. Ship only: class setup, capture (photo/video/audio/note), pupil tagging, parent share link, export. Everything else waits. Feature-freeze 10 Aug.
- **Landing page:** rebuild StoryJar.co.uk around the switcher message. Headline test: *"Seesaw got complicated. StoryJar didn't."* Add a "Switching from Seesaw?" page (comparison table, cost calculator, migration notes) — this is your best long-term SEO asset.
- **Beta pilots:** recruit 10–15 teachers from your social following as founding users, free school tier for year one in exchange for feedback + a quotable testimonial by 25 Aug. DM your most engaged followers personally.
- **Compliance groundwork (non-negotiable for UK schools):** publish a plain-English privacy policy, UK GDPR/DPA statement, data-processing agreement template, and "where is data stored" page. Teachers will be asked by their DPO; make the answer easy. Register with the ICO if you haven't.

### Phase 2 — Build the drumbeat (11 – 31 Aug)

Teachers plan their September setup in late August — this is the decision window.

- **Content cadence (3–4 posts/week on your existing social channels):** classroom-ready capture ideas, "Seesaw vs StoryJar in 30 seconds" screen recordings, honest build-in-public posts ("why I'm not adding AI"). Short video demos outperform everything else with teachers.
- **Communities (be useful first, mention StoryJar second):** UK teacher Facebook groups (Primary Teachers UK, EYFS groups), r/TeachingUK, TES forums, eyfs.info Foundation Stage Forum, #edutwitter/X and TikTok teacher-tok. One genuinely helpful post per community per week.
- **Email list:** add a "Get the September setup guide" capture on the landing page. Ship a free downloadable *"Evidence-capture setup checklist for September"* PDF — useful regardless of tool, StoryJar-branded.
- **Paid (optional, ~£150–300 of budget):** small Facebook/Instagram test targeting UK primary teacher interests, 24–31 Aug only, driving to the switcher page. Kill it if cost per signup exceeds ~£5.
- Collect pilot testimonials and 2–3 short case-study quotes; put them on the landing page by 31 Aug.

### Phase 3 — Launch window (1 – 20 Sept)

- **Launch day (Tue 1 or Wed 2 Sept):** coordinated push — announcement across all channels, pilot teachers share their experience the same day, email to the list, personal DMs to everyone who engaged in August.
- **Week 1–2:** daily quick-win content ("set up your class in 3 minutes"), respond to every comment/DM within hours — responsiveness is a feature Seesaw can't match.
- **Onboarding obsession:** watch every new signup's first session (analytics or just email them). First-week goal: teacher captures 5+ pieces of work and shares once with parents — that's your activation metric.
- **Ask for the school upsell early:** any teacher active for 2 weeks gets a personal note: "Want me to send your head a one-pager?" Write that one-pager (cost comparison vs Seesaw front and centre).

### Phase 4 — Convert & compound (21 Sept – Oct half term)

- Follow up every active teacher for the school conversation; October–November is when schools review annual subscriptions.
- Publish 2–3 SEO articles: "Seesaw alternatives UK 2026", "How much does Seesaw actually cost a primary school?", "Simple learning journals for EYFS". Low competition, high intent.
- Start a monthly "StoryJar in the classroom" email. Ask happy users for reviews on EdTech Impact and Capterra UK (where switchers research).

## 4. Budget allocation (£500 max)

Domain/hosting/email tool ~£100 · optional paid social test £150–300 · design/screen-recording tools ~£50 · reserve £50–200. Everything else is time: ~10 hrs/week Aug, ~15 hrs/week launch fortnight.

## 5. Metrics

- **By 31 Aug:** 15 pilot teachers active, 150+ email signups, landing page live with testimonials.
- **By 20 Sept:** 100 teacher signups, 40% activated (5 captures + 1 parent share in week one).
- **By half term (late Oct):** 250 teachers, 5 school-tier conversions, 3 published case studies.

If activation is under 25%, stop marketing and fix onboarding — with a free tier, retention is the whole game.

## 6. Risks

- **Beta isn't ready:** launch anyway with the narrow feature set; "simple" is the pitch, so a small surface area is on-brand. Do not slip past 1 Sept — the window closes fast.
- **Safeguarding/GDPR objections:** pre-empt with the compliance pages (Phase 1). One DPO rejection story in a Facebook group can stall adoption.
- **Seesaw switching costs:** teachers fear losing history. Offer a "fresh start for a new year" frame in September (natural reset point) and promise easy export *out* of StoryJar — trust cuts both ways.
- **Solo bandwidth:** the plan front-loads asset creation into August so September is responsive work, not production work.
